# Bank-customer-churn-analysis
## Project Overview
A comprehensive data analysis project investigating customer churn patterns in banking. This analysis identifies key factors driving customer attrition and provides actionable recommendations to reduce churn.
**Dataset:** 10,000 bank customers | **Churn Rate:** 20.38% | **Status:** ✅ Complete
## Key Findings
### 1. **Product Adoption is Critical**
- **1 Product Customers:** 27.71% churn rate 
- **4 Product Customers:** 6.61% churn rate 
- **Insight:** Customers with more products are **4.2x more loyal**
### 2. **Behavioral Factors Trump Demographics**
-  Salary difference: Only 1.6% between churned and retained
-  Credit score difference: Minimal (0.2%)
-  Activity status difference: 26% between groups (SIGNIFICANT!)
-  Satisfaction difference: 0.97 points on 5-point scale
### 3. **Satisfaction is a Strong Churn Predictor**
- Unhappy customers (Score 1): ~46% churn rate
- Happy customers (Score 5): ~5% churn rate
- **Difference:** 41 percentage points
### 4. **Geographic Variations**
- Germany: Highest churn
- France & Spain: Lower churn rates
- Regional strategies needed

##  Business Recommendations
### Priority 1: Cross-Sell Products (Highest ROI)
- **Target:** 1-product customers (5,084 customers, 50.8% of base)
- **Goal:** Move them to 2+ products
- **Impact:** Could reduce churn from 27.7% to ~6%
- **Potential:** Millions in saved revenue
### Priority 2: Improve Onboarding & Engagement
- **Problem:** Many customers remain inactive
- **Solution:** Mandatory onboarding, personalized outreach
- **Critical Period:** First 6 months (new customer risk is highest)
### Priority 3: Increase Customer Satisfaction
- **Current:** Average satisfaction only 3.0/5.0
- **Target:** Move customers to score 4+
- **Methods:** Service improvements, faster complaint resolution
### Priority 4: Focus on At-Risk Segments
- 1-product customers
- Inactive members
- Low satisfaction customers
**Expected Outcome:** 30-40% reduction in churn rate
The analysis reveals that **customer loyalty is built through engagement and satisfaction, not salary level**. Banks should focus on getting customers to use multiple products and maintaining high satisfaction scores.
